Don't forget to visit!

A majestic architectural marvel overlooking Lake Pichola, the City Palace is a grand complex of courtyards, gardens, and palaces built over 400 years. Its intricate balconies, towering domes, and stunning views reflect Udaipur's royal history.


The City Palace, located near the Pichola Lake.

Located on Jag Niwas Island in Lake Pichola, this white marble palace, now a luxury hotel, was once the summer retreat of the Mewar rulers. It appears to float on water, offering a surreal experience.


The Taj Lake Palace, located near the Pichola Lake.

Another island palace on Lake Pichola, Jag Mandir served as a royal pleasure palace. Its ornate carvings, marble structures, and tranquil surroundings make it a heritage gem.


Jag Mandir, located near the Pichola Lake.

Situated by Gangaur Ghat, this 18th-century haveli showcases Rajasthan's regal lifestyle with its grand courtyards, costumes, artifacts, and nightly cultural performances.


Bagore Ki Haveli, located near Gangaur Ghat.

A beautiful garden built for royal maidens, it features lush lawns, marble fountains, and lotus pools. Its peaceful atmosphere reflects the leisure of Udaipur’s royal women.


Saheliyon Ki Bari, located near Panchwati.
